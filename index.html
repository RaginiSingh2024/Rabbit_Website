Rabbit: Holland Lop

Main Information

  • Breed Name: Holland Lop
  • Country of Origin: Netherlands
  • Breeding Time: Mid 20th Century
  • Type: Companion Rabbit
  • Weight: 1.5 - 2 kg
  • Height: 20 - 30 cm
  • Life Span: 7 - 10 years
  • Rabbit Kit Price: $30 - $100

Description

The Holland Lop is one of the most popular domestic rabbit breeds in the world, known for its adorable floppy ears and friendly personality. It is frequently kept as a pet, particularly in households with children. Holland Lops are also shown competitively and are loved for their compact size and cute appearance.

Origins

The breed was developed in the Netherlands by Adrian de Cock in the 1950s. He aimed to combine the English Lop's ears with the French Lop's body type. The Holland Lop became an official breed in the Netherlands in 1964 and was later recognized in other parts of the world, including the United States.

Temperament

Holland Lops are gentle, affectionate, and curious. They bond closely with their owners and can be trained to use litter boxes. Their docile temperament makes them excellent pets for both children and adults. Despite their small size, they are energetic and enjoy time outside their cages to hop and explore.

Health

Holland Lops are generally healthy but can be prone to ear infections due to their floppy ears. Regular grooming and check-ups are essential. A balanced diet with hay, fresh vegetables, and clean water ensures a healthy life. Spaying or neutering is recommended for pets.

Appearance Around the World

Since its introduction outside of the Netherlands, the Holland Lop has gained worldwide popularity, especially in the United States, the UK, and Japan. Breeders around the world have maintained and improved the breed standard. Rabbit shows frequently feature this breed, and its popularity continues to rise in urban and rural homes alike.
